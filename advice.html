List of Free College Advice

"Worth Every Cent(R)"

  1. Do not bring a laptop to class (or an iPod, or a cell phone (except on silent (not vibrate, silent))). This is unless, or course, for some reason, you are required to have it.
  2. Sit in the front row. This way you will not be distracted by people on Facebook with their iPods.
  3. Take notes in a composition notebook with a pen or pencil. One notebook per class, labeled in Sharpie.
  4. Buy textbooks used online. International edition if possible. Ask the professor if the previous edition of the text is OK.
  5. No music when studying. Working, yes, studying, no.
  6. Do not read in bed. If you read in bed, set an alarm because you will fall asleep.
  7. Do not underestimate the power of a power nap. 40 minuets is the sweet spot.
  8. Read "Sleep Warrior Hacks." http://www.scribd.com/doc/50070632/sleepwarrior-sleep-hacks
  9. Run. PT. Every day do something.
  10. Eat plenty. You feel better with fuel.
  11. Drink only water, juice, milk, and coffee.
  12. Use caffeine to your advantage when possible.
  13. Waking up early to study or finish a project never works.
  14. Have some way to unwind every day (XKCD, music, student newspaper).
  15. Being stressed never helps, so don't.
  16. Deadlines sneak up on you.
  17. You will not fail if the professor knows your name.
  18. Most professors are more than happy to explain things to you if you stop by their office.
  19. To shine shoes:
    1. Clean off dirt with a damp cloth.
    2. Apply a thin coat of polish with a damp rag (old undershirt). Use small circles.
    3. Let dry.
    4. Buff with another dry rag (old undershirt #2).
    5. Buff with camel hair brush. Across the toe, up and down the sides.
  20. If you review for 10 minuets a day, you will retain more than studying for hours the night before.
  21. Never work harder than you have to to earn an 'A.'
  22. Call your mom/dad/brother. They love you and are willing to listen to you vent.
  23. It is easier to keep up than to catch up.
  24. Keep as little in your workspace as possible. No posters, bobbles, or trinkets.
  25. Your roommates love you if you have headphones and not speakers.
  26. Sleep is a tempting mistress, but she is for the weak.
  27. It is halfway over after noon on Wednesday.
  28. Collect free stuff. Every free shirt is another day to put off doing laundry.
  29. Study groups don't work.
  30. Stuck on a concept? Google and Wikipedia can probably explain it to you.
  31. There is no shame in asking for help.
  32. You can usually either:
    • Pay really good attention to the lecture and get a lot from it.
    • Take really good notes.
    Find out which is more important for each class.
  33. Get a calendar and mark every exam and due date and review it daily.
  34. Make a physical checklist of things to get done and mark of tasks as you finish them.
  35. Take showers.
  36. After working for a long time on a problem, many solutions can be reached by taking a break from it.
  37. Never write and proofread on the same day.
